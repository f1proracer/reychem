Most popular

Only Representative (OR) — UK & EU

If you're exporting chemicals to the UK or EU as a non-EU/non-UK manufacturer, you almost certainly need an Only Representative. This is the most common thing Indian exporters need from us.

What is an Only Representative?

Under REACH, the person responsible for registration must be established in the UK (for UK REACH) or in the EU/EEA (for EU REACH). As an Indian manufacturer, you're not established there — so you have two options:

  1. Ask your UK/EU importer to register (which puts a burden on them and can create friction)
  2. Appoint an Only Representative — a company established in the UK or EU that registers on your behalf

Option 2 is almost always better for your commercial relationships. When Reychem acts as your OR, your importer is relieved of their registration obligations and your product's DUIN (REACH registration number) is held centrally by us.

UK OR vs EU OR — what's the difference?

Since Brexit, UK REACH and EU REACH are separate regulations. If you want to sell into both markets, you may need separate OR appointments for each. We can act as your OR for one or both — we'll clarify what's needed based on where your products are going.

  • UK REACH OR: registered with HSE (Health & Safety Executive, UK)
  • EU REACH OR: registered with ECHA (European Chemicals Agency)
  • We hold your DUIN and manage downstream user obligations
  • Annual OR maintenance included
  • You can terminate the appointment at any time

Technical core

REACH Registration & Dossier Preparation

If your substance isn't already registered (or your tonnage has changed), you'll need to submit a registration dossier to ECHA or HSE. This is the most technically intensive part of REACH — and where our specialist network earns its keep.

What's involved in a REACH registration?

A REACH dossier is a technical package that describes a substance's identity, hazard properties, exposure scenarios, and how it can be used safely. It's submitted electronically via IUCLID software and must meet detailed ECHA/HSE requirements.

We manage the entire process — you don't need any REACH knowledge yourself:

  • Substance identity and physicochemical data gathering
  • Hazard assessment (using existing data or read-across/QSAR where possible)
  • Chemical Safety Report (CSR) where required (>10 t/yr)
  • IUCLID dossier preparation and submission
  • SIEF (Substance Information Exchange Forum) coordination if joining a joint submission
  • Letter of Access (LoA) negotiation with existing registrants where relevant
  • Communication with ECHA or HSE throughout

How much does a dossier cost?

Costs vary significantly based on tonnage band, how much existing data is available, and whether data studies need to be commissioned. We'll give you a realistic cost estimate after reviewing your substance(s). We won't surprise you mid-project.

Brexit transition

UK REACH / Brexit Transition

Brexit created a separate UK chemical regulation — UK REACH — administered by the HSE. If you were selling into the UK via EU REACH registrations, you may have a separate obligation to address.

UK REACH in a nutshell

After Brexit, the UK adopted its own version of REACH, retained in UK law. The key difference: EU REACH registrations no longer cover sales into Great Britain. UK-based importers were given transitional time to register under UK REACH, but those transition windows are closing.

As an Indian exporter, the key questions are:

  • Is your UK importer registered under UK REACH, or are they relying on a transitional arrangement that is about to expire?
  • Does your importer have a UK REACH registration (UKRIN), or do you need a UK OR?
  • Are there any substances on the UK REACH SVHC list that need authorisation?

This is an area where many Indian exporters have simply not been told about the separate UK obligation. We'll check your situation and tell you clearly what needs to happen — before a UK customs issue brings it to your attention the hard way.

Documentation

Safety Data Sheets, Labels & Supply Chain Documentation

Even if your substance is registered, your UK or EU buyer will likely ask for a compliant Safety Data Sheet (SDS) as part of the order process. Getting this wrong can hold up your first shipment.

What we produce for you

  • UK SDS: 16-section SDS compliant with UK REACH Annex II and GB CLP, in English, with the correct HSE guidance format
  • EU SDS: 16-section SDS compliant with EU REACH Annex II and EU CLP (Regulation 1272/2008), in the required language(s)
  • CLP hazard labels: UK and/or EU compliant labels with correct GHS pictograms, signal words, hazard and precautionary statements
  • Exposure Scenarios (ES): Attached to extended SDS where required (for registered substances above 10 t/yr)
  • Downstream User communication: Notification and record-keeping support for your UK/EU buyers

Why not just use a generic template?

UK and EU SDS requirements changed significantly after Brexit and are updated regularly. A generic or outdated SDS template risks non-compliance, returned shipments, or liability issues for your buyer — which damages your commercial relationship. We produce SDS documents specific to your substance, intended uses, and the correct regulatory version.
